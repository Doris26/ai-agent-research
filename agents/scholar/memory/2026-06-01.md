## Papers Found (7 new)

### 1. Compiling Agentic Workflows into LLM Weights
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22502
- **Authors:** Dennis, Patil, Shabahang, Guo (i14 / Univ. of Melbourne)
- **Date:** May 2026
- **Key finding:** External orchestrators (LangGraph, ADK, CrewAI etc.) can be compiled into fine-tuned model weights ("subterranean agents") — near-frontier quality at ~100x lower cost for procedural tasks
- **GCP angle:** ADK explicitly named in the paper. Vertex AI Distillation could be the delivery mechanism. Major cost story competitive pressure on Agent Engine.
- **Action:** Posted to #scholar-feed. Flagged to Analyst as top-priority deep dive.

### 2. AgentTrust: Runtime Safety Evaluation and Interception
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04785
- **Authors:** Chenglin Yang
- **Date:** May 6, 2026
- **Key finding:** Pre-execution tool call interceptor with shell deobfuscation, SafeFix (safer alternatives), RiskChain (multi-step attack chain detection). Works across 14 frameworks.
- **GCP angle:** ADK Model Armor is prompt-level only. No pre-execution tool interceptor with deobfuscation in GEAP. Gap confirmed.

### 3. Agent-ValueBench
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10365
- **Authors:** Dong, Feng, Jiang, Ye, Zhang, Song
- **Date:** May 11, 2026
- **Key finding:** Agent values diverge from underlying LLM values. 4,335 value-conflict tasks across 394 environments. LLM safety evals do NOT transfer to deployed agents.
- **GCP angle:** GEAP Agent Engine has no agent-native values evaluation layer. Critical gap for compliance-sensitive deployments.
- **Action:** Flagged to Analyst as second top-priority deep dive.

### 4. Predictive Maps of Multi-Agent Reasoning (Successor Representation)
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11453
- **Authors:** Park, Alharthi
- **Date:** May 2026
- **Key finding:** Spectral diagnostic for multi-agent topology selection using successor representation. Maps spectral radius/gap/condition number to failure modes: drift amplification, consensus failure, perturbation sensitivity.
- **GCP angle:** ADK supports star/pipeline/custom topologies but no diagnostic. Math foundation for an ADK Topology Advisor tool.

### 5. ROME + ARISE: Enhancing Agent Safety Judgment for Deceptive OOD Scenarios
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03242
- **Date:** May 2026
- **Key finding:** ROME rewrites 100 unsafe trajectories → 300 deceptive evaluation instances. Frontier models still fail on hidden-risk and contextually ambiguous cases. ARISE injects analogical safety trajectories at inference time to improve.
- **GCP angle:** ADK Evaluation Layer uses standard safety categories. Adversarial OOD rewriting should be added as evaluation mode.

### 6. Taxonomy and Consistency Analysis of Safety Benchmarks
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16282
- **Authors:** Li, Fung, Li, Ismail, Iqbal (McGill/Kean/Zayed)
- **Date:** May 2026
- **Key finding:** 40 safety benchmarks cataloged, six-axis taxonomy. Most have inconsistent threat models and incompatible metrics — published safety scores not directly comparable across benchmarks.
- **GCP angle:** Agent Engine 13-dimension evaluator set needs auditing against this taxonomy.

### 7. Do Agents Need to Plan Step-by-Step?
- **arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08477
- **Authors:** Otani, Bhutani, Kim, Zhang, Hruschka (CMU/Apple)
- **Date:** May 8, 2026 (ACM AIES May 2026)
- **Key finding:** For data-centric tasks, full-horizon planning with lazy replanning = accuracy parity with step-by-step at lower LLM invocation count. Step-by-step only needed under high environmental uncertainty.
- **GCP angle:** Direct ADK cost optimization for BigQuery/Spanner data agents.
